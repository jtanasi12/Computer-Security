RSA

Algorithm

RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is a widely used asymmetric cryptographic algorithm for secure data transmission over the internet. It relies on the difficulty of factoring large prime numbers to encrypt and decrypt messages, ensuring confidentiality and authentication in digital communication.

Double Transposition

In a double transposition cipher, the message is scrambled twice using different patterns, enhancing its encryption strength. This double layer of rearrangement adds complexity and makes it more challenging for unauthorized parties to decipher the encrypted message without the proper decryption key.
